# Design Pillars

> 3 non-negotiables. Every feature must serve a pillar, or it gets cut.

## 1. Glance to Read, Grind to Master
Arcade clarity on the surface, real depth underneath. Everything reads at a glance —
five honest stat bars, no tuning menus, no hidden math — but mastery runs deep:
surfaces, brand quirks, the perfect line. You don't upgrade a car, you **choose** one
and learn to own it.
- **Serves:** arcade clarity, fixed-stat cars, depth-through-mastery, readable surfaces/grip.
- **Vetoes:** setup/tuning screens, stat-grind, illegible systems, filler cars, shallow
  mechanics with nothing to chew on.

## 2. Arcade Rally Flow
The drive has to feel *alive* in your hands and put you in the zone — buttery, weighty,
responsive handling that never hitches, on rally terrain that keeps shifting under you
(surfaces, jumps, slipstream, weather). Feel and framerate are one promise: if it isn't
smooth, nothing else matters.
- **Serves:** feel-first, performance-first, mixed surfaces, verticality/jumps, slipstream,
  risk/reward racing.
- **Vetoes:** mushy or floaty handling, anything that drops frames, friction that breaks
  flow, hold-accelerate-to-win tracks.

## 3. Boss-to-Boss Career
A lenient class-climbing progression, NFS:MW-shaped but **mechanical, not narrative**. Beat each
chapter's boss to pink-slip their car and climb toward the top class. The hook is the *chase* —
better cars, harder rivals, the next unlock — not a story. The door's always open: lose a race, you
still inch forward. **No narrative in v1** (characters/story deferred).
- **Serves:** boss-to-boss progression, pink-slip car unlocks, class climb,
  lenient/never-hard-walled progression, desirable/distinct rival cars.
- **Vetoes:** win-to-progress gates, punishing dead-ends, bloated career layers, and — for now —
  **lore/story/character work** (deferred, not this build).

## Anti-pillars (explicitly NOT making)
- A sim or tuning-heavy racer — no setups, no min-maxing.
- A grind/frustration ladder that walls out the player.
- A graphics-fidelity showcase that costs framerate.
- A **story-driven** racer (v1 has no narrative — VHS is style, not fiction).
- A **style filter that costs readability or feel** — the VHS look decorates the game, it never
  fights the driving or fogs a corner.
