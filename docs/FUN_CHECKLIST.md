# Fun Validation Checklist

> The industry gold standard isn't a framework — it's a **practice**: prototype fast,
> playtest, watch where players struggle/quit, iterate. **Find the fun early.**
> The lenses below are how you *read* a playtest, not a substitute for doing one.

## The practice (non-negotiable)

1. **Prototype** the smallest playable version.
2. **Play it** (and get others to, when possible).
3. **Observe** honestly — where does it drag, confuse, or bore?
4. **Iterate** on that. If a feature can't pass the lenses after a few tries, cut it.

If it isn't fun in the prototype, more content won't save it. Bail or rework early.

## The 3 diagnostic lenses (primary)

Run a feature/build against these. They're questions, not checkboxes to game.

### 🎮 Game Feel — *does it feel good in the hands?*
- Does input respond instantly and readably (no mushy lag)?
- Does the car have believable weight, momentum, traction?
- Does every action get tactile feedback (camera, particles, sound, shake)?

### 🌊 Flow — *is challenge matched to skill?*
- Is it too easy (boring) or too hard (frustrating) right now?
- Are goals clear and feedback immediate?
- Does difficulty rise as the player improves?

### 🤔 Interesting Decisions — *are the choices meaningful?*
- Does the player make real trade-offs (brake point, line, drift vs grip, boost timing)?
- Is there an obviously-dominant option? (If yes, it's a chore, not a decision — fix it.)

## Supporting lenses (when relevant)

- **Theory of Fun (longevity):** once the player masters this, what new pattern is next?
  If nothing, that's where staleness will hit.
- **MDA (vocabulary):** name the target *aesthetic/feeling* first, then check your
  *mechanics* are likely to produce it. Don't confuse "added a handbrake" with
  "cornering feels thrilling."
- **Schell's Lenses:** the reference book to dip into when a problem resists the above.

## Verdict
After a playtest, answer one question in plain words: **was that fun, and why / why not?**
Log it in [PLAYTESTS.md](PLAYTESTS.md). Honesty here is the whole point.
